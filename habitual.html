Habitual - We got what you love!

The challenge


The goal was to design an application Habitual. Habitual is an e-commerce mobile application that will allow users to buy items they love. The user can enter their interests and based off on their interests and search results, Habitual will recommend items for them to buy from.

The roles I played from the beginning till the end of this project

  • User Researcher
  • Data Analyst
  • UI/UX Designer
  • Product Designer

The Journey


I started the process with a session of brainstorming. Any kind of ideas, design, flows whatever that came up to my mind, I jotted and sketched them down. Every idea and inspiration was welcome here. It was just to get my mind working towards a pool of ideas that I can work from.


My initial sketches and ideas for Habitual

User flows


After the brainstorming session, I created some user flows to have an outline of how the app would work. They are the best way to solve the issue of communicating the way we want our product to work and the way users really intereact with our products. The help us answer three simple questions

  1. What is the goal of the flow
  2. Who is doing it
  3. And how do they do it

Sitemap


Sitemaps are hierarchical diagrams that show how pages are organized or prioritized. I drew up a sitemap for the app to give it a structure to help me plan out the different navigation schemes within the app.

It is also beneficial in figuring out hat pages you need to show at certain times, like what pages of your website are public to every user or if it is only available when logged in or if it is just for the administrations.


A sitemap for Habitual

Wireframes


Wireframing presents the information that will be displayed on the page in a more detailed manner than the sketches and userflows.

I drew up some wireframes for the mains screens that I wanted to construct with more details and made a very basic prototype showing the flow and working of the application.


Wireframes for Habitual

Deciding the aesthetic of Habitual


I wanted the the application to look very friendly and inviting, since it is and e-commerce platform and essentially users would be using it to buy things.


Colour Palette


I tested out different colours, what would look inviting, what would be not be harsh towards the eyes and what would not look daunting with images, buttons and texts.

I decided on the below palette. I kept the blacks warm to compliment the mustard yellow look of the app giving it an overall warm look and good saturation contrast.


Typography


For typography, I picked out "Lato". It had different style variations and looked clear and neat on large and small fonts.


I tested out all the font sizes and font weights I would use in the application to check if they would sit well.

Logo


For the logo design, I went with the simple shopping bag with a smiley face on it showing Habitual's warmth and happiness in helping you find the right product for yourself. The shopping bag is in the general yellow colour of the application.

Bringing It All Together


Onboarding Screens


I wanted the onboarding screens to be very cheerful and interesting with a little bit of eye catching characters to interest the users. Every screen explains a little bit about the application keeping it simple and short yet informative for what the user is signing upto and to give them a clear idea of what the website is offering them. Users will not sign up for a site they don't understand or find appealing.

The user can sign up or log in if they are a returning customer or skip the whole onboarding screen and move directly to the homepage of Habitual.



Onboarding prototype



Login Screen


The user can login into their account to see more features offered by Habitual like suggestions made by Habitual or see thier own interests in "Your interests" tab.

I kept the signup and login procedure short and without asking too much details since it has been a proven UX problem that people abandon forms that ask too much information and are an effort to fill up. I have given them a choice to either just use their email address and password to sign up / log in or through connecting thier different social media accounts which only takes a click!



Login prototype



Searching For A Product


It's easy to now narrow down your search results with filters. Category search , price range filter, staff picks, sales, customer review, etc all can narrow down your efforts in finding the item that you want. It's very easy, simple and efficient.



Search prototype



Add To Cart


After picking out your favorite item, the user can add the item to their cart to purchase it later or at that moment. After adding the item to the cart, a red bouncy dot will appear on the shopping bag at the upper right corner of the screen indicating the user that they have an item in the bag.



Add to Cart prototype



Purchase An Item


The checkout procedure is simple and easy to use. The details have already been filled in if it is a returning customer to not bog down the procedure. After clicking on checkout, confirming their details, the user gets a short animation indicating that the purchase was a success.

I have used visual representations and animations in the app because they are fun to see and also grabs the user's attention but I have also kept in mind not to overwhelm the users with too much sensory overloading.



Purchase an Item prototype



What I learned


Designing a complete application from scratch taught me a lot of things. It taught me how great ideas come and go so easily. When you start working on an idea that you thought was so great, turns out when you implement it, it usually does not fit in well with the flow.

This project taught me how easy and important it is to get feedback. Whenever I got stuck in making a decision, whenever I got lost in the "UX designer thinking", I always went out and asked some opinions from family and friends just to get a user's perspective and not a designer's one; Afterall the app is being designed for customers like them.

Most importantly, I found myself having fun when designing Habitual. It was a fun, cheery project and above all, I got to polish my Figma and prototyping skills!

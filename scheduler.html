Scheduler : Track Timetables

Main objective


Scheduler was my Final Year Project for my BSCS degree program. We designed this website for FAST National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences.

The team


I worked with two amazing partners and human beings, M. Talha Abbasi and Rida Tariq who taught me a lot along the way and were amazing project partners to work with.

The roles I played from the beginning till the end of this project

  • User Researcher
  • Data Analyst
  • UI/UX Designer
  • Product Designer
  • Developer

Where it all began


Before we coded, we sat down and sketched ideas for how we wanted our website to look. Since the purpose of Scheduler was to create timetables by taking in constraints put in by the admin, we agreed to design it in a professional look with a sleek design. The targeted audience for this website was only the administration so it was easy to narrow it down to a specific type of audience.


Our very basic initial planned screens

Iterating the design


Throughout the project development period our deign remained pretty much the same. We added more pages that were required for data collection for the constraints and we also decided to show the generated timetable in an exported csv file too.


From scratch to prototyping


Prototype



Now, designing the generated timetable took a lot of brainstorming. We couldn't decide how to show the time table. Should we go for a list? Different tabs? Dropboxes that would take the day, batch and section as input?.

In the end we decided to go with a graphical view of a timetable, so the user can be familiar with it like they see their day planners or calendars. The different colours show the batch representation and the viewer can pick the day of the week who's schedule they wanted to see.

It taught me with hard facts to design what I can develop and I learned that an idea formed in my head can be a lot challenging to actually form through design and development.
